Shahrukh Khan

Biography

Shahrukh Khan, also known as the "King of Bollywood", is an Indian actor, film producer, and television personality. He was born on November 2, 1965, in New Delhi, India.

Early Life

Shahrukh Khan grew up in a middle-class family and attended St. Columba's School in Delhi. He later pursued his Bachelor's degree in Economics from Hansraj College and then completed his Master's degree in Mass Communication from Jamia Millia Islamia University.

Career

Shahrukh Khan made his acting debut in the television series "Fauji" in 1988. He gained popularity with his breakthrough role in the film "Deewana" in 1992. Since then, he has appeared in numerous successful films, including "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge", "Kuch Kuch Hota Hai", "Devdas", "Chennai Express", and many more.

Awards and Achievements

Shahrukh Khan has received several awards and accolades throughout his career, including 14 Filmfare Awards. He has also been honored with the Padma Shri by the Government of India for his contributions to Indian cinema.

Personal Life

Shahrukh Khan is married to Gauri Khan, and they have three children together. He is known for his philanthropic work and has supported various causes over the years.
